When these distinctions collapse, artificial intelligence contact becomes unstable, misleading, extractive, generic, or falsely sourced.

## Failure Mode 4: Winner-Frame

Winner-frame occurs when artificial intelligence contact is reduced to a competition among models, companies, providers, platforms, or major players.

Winner-frame says:

**Which AI wins?**

But within AI Foundations, the deeper question is not which player wins the colosseum.

The deeper question is:

**What happens when artificial intelligence contact becomes a major human experience layer?**

Winner-frame fails because it looks only at visible players.

It measures compute, scale, funding, capability, market share, product adoption, and institutional competition.

Those may matter at the model and product layer.

They do not define the architecture of contact.

A model can win a benchmark and still fail contact.

A company can win distribution and still fail source-line governance.

A product can win adoption and still collapse user, model, interface, and contact.

Winner-frame fails because it crowns players before locating Source.
